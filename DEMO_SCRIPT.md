# SayangASD — Demo Script (~20 minutes)

---

## BRIEF INTRODUCTION (2 minutes)

> *Open the app at `http://localhost:5050`. Keep the Demo Nav button visible in the bottom-right corner throughout.*

**Speaker:**

"Our solution is **SayangASD** — a lightweight mobile-first platform designed for B40 families in rural Johor who are raising children with autism spectrum disorder, or ASD.

The core problem we are solving is this: ASD early intervention works, but in the Global South it breaks down at the last mile. Caregivers in rural areas cannot reach specialists. Community health workers have no digital tool to track children's progress. Families are too ashamed — or too uninformed — to apply for the welfare support they are entitled to. And all of this is made worse by unreliable internet.

SayangASD connects three stakeholder groups around a single child's data: the **caregiver** at home, the **community health worker** (CHW) in the field, and the **village head** acting as a trusted community bridge to government welfare. The platform works offline-first, stores data locally, and syncs when connectivity returns.

We will now walk you through three realistic scenarios. Use the Demo Nav button to switch between user personas."

## SCENARIO 1 — Caregiver records a therapy session and contacts the specialist (2 minutes)

> *Click Demo Nav → under 👩 Siew Hua (Caregiver) → click 🏠 Home*

### i) User Role
**Siew Hua** is a mother in Kluang, Johor. Her son Darren is 6 years old and has been diagnosed with ASD. She is the primary caregiver and the one who runs daily home therapy activities prescribed by the specialist. She owns a basic Android smartphone and has limited but functional mobile data coverage at home.

### ii) User's Goal
Darren has a scheduled joint-attention exercise today (*Latihan Perhatian Bersama*). Siew Hua needs to:
1. Log how each of the five attempts went
2. Record Darren's mood during the session
3. Save the session result
4. Send a progress update to Dr. Arina Zulkifli at NASOM JB

### iii) Steps Taken Within the System

> *Point to the home screen at `/`*

The home screen shows Siew Hua the current date, Darren's program week (Week 14), his overall completion rate (78%), and today's three scheduled activities. The first activity — *Latihan Perhatian Bersama* — is marked **Hari ini** (today). She taps it.

> *Click Demo Nav → 🗣️ Therapy Session (`/therapy`)*

The therapy screen loads with the audio instruction guide active. A transcript is displayed: "Take the red ball, show it to Darren and say 'bola'. Wait for him to look at your face before continuing."

For each of the five attempts she taps:
- ✅ if Darren made clear eye contact
- 🔶 if the response was partial
- ❌ if there was no response

She then selects his mood from the five-emoji scale at the bottom (😢 → 😄).

**Inputs required:**
- 5 × attempt result (check / partial / cross)
- 1 × mood selection

> *Interact: tap ✅ and 🔶 on several attempts, select 🙂, then tap 'Simpan & Selesai'*

### iv) Information Generated by the System

> *The result page at `/therapy/result` loads automatically*

The result screen displays:
- **Score:** 4/5 eye contact attempts
- **Mood:** 🙂
- **Duration:** 9 minutes
- **14-day trend bar chart** showing Darren's daily eye contact score — an upward trend is visible

A status banner confirms: *"Data saved locally. Will sync to cloud when online."*

> *Click Demo Nav → 💬 Specialist Chat (`/chat`)*

The chat screen opens showing the async thread with Dr. Arina. The system has already attached today's session report as a structured message bubble — score, mood, duration, and activity name — timestamped 09:41. A pending notice shows Dr. Arina typically replies within 24 hours.

### v) How the System Supports Decision-Making

Siew Hua can see immediately whether today's session met the target. The 14-day trend chart shows her whether Darren's progress is improving or plateauing — information she could not easily track with paper notes. Dr. Arina receives structured, timestamped clinical data rather than a vague WhatsApp message, enabling her to give specific, informed feedback without an in-person visit. The specialist consult happens asynchronously, which is the only realistic model for a family 80 km from the nearest NASOM centre.

## SCENARIO 2 — CHW conducts a milestone assessment and generates a referral report (2 minutes)

> *Click Demo Nav → under 🏥 Fadzli (CHW) → 📋 Milestone Assessment (`/milestone`)*

### i) User Role
**Fadzli bin Nordin** is a Community Health Worker covering rural villages in Daerah Kluang. He visits assigned families once a month. He is not a clinician but is trained to observe and record developmental milestones in young children with suspected developmental delays. He carries a low-end Android phone and frequently works in areas with no mobile coverage.

### ii) User's Goal
Fadzli is visiting the Hassan family today to assess Ahmad, who is 4 years old and has been flagged by his kindergarten teacher as showing possible signs of ASD. Fadzli needs to:
1. Complete a standardised developmental checklist based on what he observed during the visit
2. Generate a report with a clear verdict
3. Submit the report to the district hospital specialist for review

### iii) Steps Taken Within the System

> *Point to the milestone screen*

The form presents eight developmental items split into two domains:

**Social (4 items):**
- Responds to name when called
- Eye contact during interaction
- Interest in other children
- Imaginative / pretend play

**Communication (4 items):**
- Two-word phrases
- Points to objects to show interest
- Follows two-step instructions
- Understands simple questions

For each item, Fadzli marks ✅ (2 pts), 🔶 partial (1 pt), or ❌ not yet (0 pts), based on direct observation during his home visit.

**Inputs required:**
- 8 × milestone rating (check / partial / cross)

> *Interact: click a realistic mix — some ✅, some 🔶, several ❌ — then tap 'Jana Laporan'*

### iv) Information Generated by the System

The form is replaced by a generated report showing:
- **Overall score and percentage** (e.g. 45%)
- **Verdict banner** — colour-coded:
  - 🟢 Green: ≥ 70% — Good progress, continue monitoring
  - 🟡 Amber: 40–69% — Needs attention, review intervention plan
  - 🔴 Red: < 40% — Urgent referral required
- **Category breakdown:** separate progress bars for Social and Communication scores
- **Action button:** either "Hantar ke Pakar untuk Semakan" (review) or "Hantar Rujukan Segera ke Hospital" (urgent)

> *Tap the referral/review button — an alert confirms the report has been sent with a reference number*

### v) How the System Supports Decision-Making

Fadzli does not need to interpret the raw score himself — the system applies the rubric and tells him what action to take. A red verdict triggers an urgent hospital referral button directly on screen; he does not need to know which hospital or which department. The system generates a reference number (e.g. HSA-20260519-0089) that the family can quote when they arrive. This replaces a paper form that previously took two weeks to reach the specialist by post.

## SCENARIO 3 — Village head identifies financial aid and sends a discrete referral (2 minutes)

> *Click Demo Nav → under 🏘️ Dato' Razak (Village Head) → 💰 Financial Aid (`/aid`)*

### i) User Role
**Dato' Razak** is the *Ketua Kampung* — the elected village head of a rural community in Kluang. He is a trusted figure in the community. Families approach him when they need help but are too embarrassed to go directly to government offices. He is not a welfare officer, but he knows his community, understands who qualifies for support, and has a relationship with the JKM (Jabatan Kebajikan Masyarakat) district office.

### ii) User's Goal
The Hassan family — parents of Ahmad (the child from Scenario 2) — has approached Dato' Razak privately. They are a B40 household earning RM 1,200/month. They need financial support to cover Darren's therapy costs but are afraid of social stigma (*malu*) if their neighbours learn that their child has a disability. Dato' Razak needs to:
1. Identify welfare schemes Ahmad's family qualifies for
2. Submit a referral to JKM **without** exposing the child's diagnosis or clinical records to anyone
3. Protect the family's dignity in the community

### iii) Steps Taken Within the System

> *Point to the aid directory at `/aid`*

The directory displays four welfare schemes available in Daerah Kluang — all stored offline and last updated 18 May 2026:

| Scheme | Amount | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Bantuan OKU | RM 300 / month | ✅ Layak |
| Elaun Penjagaan OKU | RM 450 / month | ✅ Layak |
| Terapi Disubsidi NASOM | Up to 80% subsidy | Semak |
| Tabung Kebajikan ASD Johor | RM 100–500 | Opens July |

Two schemes are pre-flagged as eligible based on the family's income and OKU status. Dato' Razak taps **Jana Rujukan**.

> *Tap 'Jana Rujukan' → the referral screen at `/referral` loads*

The **Privacy Wall** banner activates at the top of the screen. The form shows only:
- Family surname: Hassan bin Malik
- Last 4 digits of IC: ●●●● – ●●●● – 5231
- District: Kluang, Johor
- Household income: RM 1,200 / bulan (B40 ✓)
- Reason (generic): Family requires financial guidance and welfare support for a child with disability
- Clinical data included: ❌ Not permitted by system

**Inputs required:** Review and confirm pre-filled form (no data entry needed from Dato' Razak)

> *Tap 'Hantar Rujukan ke JKM Johor'. The success confirmation screen appears.*

### iv) Information Generated by the System

The confirmation screen displays:
- ✅ Referral sent successfully
- **Reference number:** JKM-KLG-20260519-0042
- Note: JKM officer will contact the family within 5–7 working days
- Confirmation that **no medical data was transmitted**
- Confirmation that the referral uses the existing JKM Johor intake channel — no new institutional agreement required

### v) How the System Supports Decision-Making

Dato' Razak has completed a welfare referral without:
- Requiring the family to travel to any government office
- Exposing Ahmad's diagnosis to the community
- Needing clinical knowledge himself
- Setting up any new inter-agency agreement

The Privacy Wall is not merely a UI choice — it enforces a data minimisation principle compatible with Malaysia's PDPA 2010. The family's dignity is protected because the referral carries no clinical label. Neighbours will not know why JKM contacted the family. This directly addresses the most cited barrier to welfare uptake in rural Malaysian ASD families: social shame.

## EDGE CASES AND SYSTEM LIMITATIONS (3 minutes)

> *Click Demo Nav → ⚠️ Sync Conflict (`/conflict`), then 📵 Offline Mode (`/offline`) for reference*

### Edge Case 1 — Conflicting data from multiple devices

> *Navigate to `/conflict`*

In rural households, multiple caregivers sometimes use different devices — a mother and a grandmother both logging sessions for the same child while offline. When they reconnect, the system detects a conflict: two records for the same date with different eye-contact scores (4/5 vs 2/5).

**Smart-Sync protocol:**
1. All sessions from both devices are **preserved** (union merge — no data is discarded)
2. For the specific conflicting field, the **more recent timestamp wins** (08:30 beats 07:55)
3. A conflict log is sent to the CHW for manual review

**Limitation acknowledged:** If a caregiver manually enters a wrong date — back-dating a session, for example — the timestamp logic cannot detect that error. The more recent timestamp will still win, even if it is wrong. Correction requires CHW intervention. This is a known constraint we flag openly.

### Edge Case 2 — Incomplete or incorrect milestone data

If Fadzli submits a milestone form with all answers set to *partial* — either because he did not observe clearly or because he was uncertain — the scoring system produces a mid-range result (50%) that looks clinically plausible but may be meaningless.

**Current mitigation:** The system requires every field to be answered before submission — no blanks allowed. A future mitigation would be a per-item confidence flag ("I did not directly observe this") and a backend flag alerting the specialist when a submission has an unusually uniform distribution of answers.

**Broader system limitation:** SayangASD amplifies human effort — it does not replace it. If CHW visit frequency drops due to staff turnover, transport budgets, or community distrust, the data becomes stale and trend analysis loses clinical validity. Low community participation is not a technical problem the app can solve on its own; it requires the NGO operator to maintain field relationships. We consider this an honest constraint of any community-based digital health tool in the Global South.

## DISCUSSION (2 minutes)

> *Open to TA questions. Prepared responses below:*

**"Why not just use WhatsApp?"**
WhatsApp is unstructured, unsearchable, and puts the cognitive burden on the doctor to parse messages. SayangASD generates machine-readable records that feed into a longitudinal dashboard. A therapist seeing 40 children cannot triage 40 voice notes.

**"Is this scalable beyond one family?"**
Yes. The CHW view is designed to show a list of all assigned children — that dashboard screen is a Phase 2 deliverable. The backend is stateless per session; scaling is a deployment question, not an architectural constraint.

**"What about data ownership?"**
Data is stored on-device first. The family owns the local copy. Cloud sync is opt-in. We intentionally avoided a centralised database in this prototype to reduce the data governance burden on the NGO operator.

**"What is missing from this prototype?"**
Three things we acknowledge honestly:
1. Push notifications to alert Siew Hua when Dr. Arina replies
2. A CHW dashboard listing all assigned children with status
3. A real authentication layer (current demo assumes device = user)

These are Phase 2 features. They do not affect the core data flows demonstrated today.
